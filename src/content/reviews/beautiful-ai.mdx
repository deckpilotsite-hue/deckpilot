## Overview

Beautiful.ai is a presentation platform built around **Smart Slides**: layouts that auto-adjust, resize, and align as you add or edit content so you focus on the story. It targets professional business decks with strong brand controls, shared workspaces, and viewer analytics. In 2026 it offers full-deck AI generation, AI text and image tools, and an early-access API. Pricing: Pro ($12/mo annually), Team ($40–50/user/mo), Enterprise; 14-day trial (credit card required). SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, CCPA, and clear AI data policies (no training on customer data) make it a fit for regulated or brand-sensitive teams.

## Smart Slides and design automation

### How Smart Slides work

Smart Slides apply layout and typography rules automatically: add a bullet or change text and the slide adapts. Hundreds of layouts; support for tables, charts, timelines, org charts, and word clouds. Themes and templates keep decks visually consistent; the editor has been updated for a faster workflow.

### Collaboration and export

Live editing, comments, and version history (Pro: per-slide revert, recover deleted slides). Export to editable PPTX, PDF, and Google Slides; the PowerPoint add-in brings Smart Slide technology into PowerPoint.

## AI and integrations

### Generative AI

Multiple models (Gemini, Claude for text; Imagen, DALL·E for images). AI presentation generation is step-by-step and outline-oriented rather than single prompt-to-deck. The API (early access) can generate full, editable presentations via POST with Bearer token auth.

### Integrations

Slack, Salesforce, Monday.com, ChatGPT (custom GPT), PowerPoint add-in, and Webex.

## Verdict

Beautiful.ai is strongest when you need **fast, board- or client-ready decks** with **consistent branding** and **share links plus engagement analytics**. Good for sales, marketing, and internal reporting where throughput and brand governance matter. Weaker when you need highly custom, pixel-level layout or strict PowerPoint-centric import/export fidelity. User ratings are high on product-focused sites (e.g. G2 4.7), lower where billing and support dominate.
