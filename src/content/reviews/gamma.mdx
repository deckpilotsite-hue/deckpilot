## Overview

Gamma is a cloud-based AI design platform for presentations, documents, and web pages. It positions itself as an “AI design partner”: you provide a prompt or content (e.g. outline or Word doc), and Gamma’s AI generates a coherent, visually styled deck using a **card-based** format that adapts to content length—more like a responsive document than fixed 16:9 slides. The platform grew rapidly after an AI pivot in March 2023 (e.g. 50M+ users, 250M+ “gammas” by 2025) and in 2025 introduced the **Gamma Agent** for web search, restyling, and feedback, plus a public API for automation. Plans range from Free (basic limits, 400 credits) to Plus (~$8–10/user/mo) and Pro (~$20/user/mo) with unlimited generation, real-time collaboration, custom themes, and API access. Gamma suits non-designers who need pitch decks, marketing content, or internal docs quickly; strengths are speed and ease, while export fidelity to PowerPoint/Google Slides can require manual fixes.

## Features and workflow

Gamma creates from a prompt or from uploaded content; it often builds an outline first, then designs slides. Themes and Smart Diagrams keep branding consistent. The editor is slash-command and Notion-like; you can ask AI to rewrite or visualize per slide. Export to PDF, PPTX, and PNG is supported; interactive elements (videos, embeds) flatten in static exports. Real-time co-editing and analytics are on paid plans. Security includes SOC 2 Type II, encryption, and a GDPR-ready DPA; content is not used for AI training when you opt out. The API (GA late 2025) supports generating gammas from code and integrates with Zapier and Make.

## Verdict

Gamma is **strongest for rapid ideation**: going from idea to a coherent, on-brand deck faster than traditional slide tools. It’s a good fit for startups, marketers, and educators who accept a web-native, card-style workflow and can tolerate export cleanup. It’s less suitable when you need exact 16:9 fidelity, heavy offline use, or maximum design freedom. Try the Free plan and test PPTX export with your use case before committing.
