## Quick verdict

**AiPPT** generates slide decks in seconds from a text prompt, uploaded document (Word, PDF), or URL. It offers 200,000+ templates, AI image generation (Nano Banana), and brand controls; export (PPTX/PDF) requires a paid plan. **Gamma** creates full decks from a prompt or document using a card-based, web-native format—content and design in one flow. It has a generous free tier (400 credits), real-time collaboration on paid plans, and custom branding/API on Pro. Choose AiPPT for one-click generation and massive template variety with doc/URL input; choose Gamma for team collaboration, modern web output, and a single AI-driven workflow from idea to deck.

## Key differences

- **Input flexibility:** AiPPT = prompt, document (Word, PDF), or webpage URL; good for turning existing content into slides. Gamma = prompt or uploaded doc; outline-first, then full deck.
- **Format:** AiPPT = traditional 16:9 slides; export to PPTX/PDF. Gamma = card-based, scrollable “gammas”; export to PPTX/PDF/PNG, but PPTX can need reformatting.
- **Templates & design:** AiPPT = 200k+ templates, drag-and-drop edit, brand colors/fonts/logo. Gamma = theme-based layouts, fewer freeform options; Pro adds custom branding and fonts.
- **Pricing:** AiPPT Free = 6 total AI generations (max 2/day), no export without paid; Plus ~$9/mo, Pro ~$12/mo (more generations and image credits). Gamma Free = 400 credits (~10 decks); Plus ~$8–10/mo, Pro ~$20/mo (collaboration, API, analytics).
- **Collaboration:** AiPPT = shareable links; real-time co-editing is limited. Gamma = real-time co-editing on paid plans; Team/Business add SSO and workspace templates.

## When to choose AiPPT

- You often start from existing material: notes, articles, or documents that you want turned into slides quickly.
- You want a huge template library (200k+) and in-slide AI image generation (Nano Banana) for variety.
- You’re solo or small team and don’t need real-time co-editing; sharing via link is enough.
- Best for: busy professionals, educators, and anyone who wants “content in → slides out” with minimal steps and is okay paying for export.

## When to choose Gamma

- You want one flow from prompt or doc to a polished-looking deck with themes and images handled by AI.
- You need real-time collaboration, workspace templates, and (on Pro) API, analytics, and custom branding.
- You’re fine with a card-based, web-publishable format and possible PPTX cleanup after export.
- Best for: startups, consultants, and teams that value speed plus collaboration and a modern output format.

## Bottom line

**AiPPT** excels at fast, input-flexible generation and template variety; its main drawback is paywalled export and limited collaboration. **Gamma** excels at AI-first workflow, collaboration, and a consistent “design partner” experience; its main drawback is less freeform control and export quirks. Use AiPPT when your source is often a document or URL and you want maximum template choice; use Gamma when you want team features and a single, coherent AI-generated deck.
