## Quick verdict

**Canva** is a full design suite: Magic Design generates slide drafts from prompts in ~20–60 seconds, and you then edit everything freely with a huge library of templates, images, and elements. Brand Kit applies colors automatically (logos and fonts are manual). Output can be generic; PPTX export often needs cleanup. **Beautiful.ai** is presentation-focused: Smart Slides auto-adjust layout as you add content, so slides stay consistent with minimal pixel-pushing. It offers viewer analytics (Pro), PowerPoint import/export, and strong brand controls, but less creative freedom. Choose Canva for versatility and full control; choose Beautiful.ai for “set it and forget it” consistency and engagement metrics.

## Key differences

- **Scope:** Canva = presentations plus social, video, docs, and more; Beautiful.ai = presentations and slide-centric workflows (sales, marketing, training).
- **Design model:** Canva = freeform WYSIWYG; you can change every element after AI generation. Beautiful.ai = template-driven; Smart Slides enforce alignment and consistency—less flexibility, fewer “messy” slides.
- **AI role:** Canva = Magic Design creates draft decks from a short prompt; Magic Write, Magic Media, and Magic Switch support text and images. Beautiful.ai = AI assists within Smart Slide templates; prompt-to-deck and API exist but the product is built around templates, not full generative drafts.
- **Pricing:** Canva Free = limited Magic Design (~10 decks/mo); Pro ~$15–16/mo for unlimited AI and Brand Kit. Beautiful.ai = no permanent free tier; Pro $12/mo (annual), Team $40–50/user/mo; 14-day trial.
- **Export & analytics:** Canva exports PDF, PPTX, MP4, etc.; PPTX often misaligns. Beautiful.ai Pro offers editable PPTX export and viewer analytics (views, completion, time per slide); PowerPoint add-in available.

## When to choose Canva

- You need one platform for slides, social, video, and other visual content; presentations are one use case among many.
- You want to tweak every element: full drag-and-drop, huge asset library, and Brand Kit (with manual logo/font placement).
- You’re okay editing AI-generated copy and fixing or avoiding PPTX export (e.g. use PDF or present from Canva).
- Best for: solo creators, marketing teams, educators who value flexibility and already use Canva.

## When to choose Beautiful.ai

- You want slides that stay visually consistent with minimal layout work; Smart Slides handle alignment and resizing.
- Viewer analytics matter for follow-up (who opened, completion rate, time per slide).
- You work in PowerPoint often: import/export and add-in are core to your workflow.
- Best for: sales and marketing teams, anyone who prioritizes “board-ready” consistency and measurable sharing over maximum creative freedom.

## Bottom line

**Canva** wins on breadth and freedom: one suite for many formats, full control after generation, at the cost of more manual editing and export quirks. **Beautiful.ai** wins on slide-specific consistency and analytics: less flexibility, but faster to a polished, measurable deck. Use Canva when design versatility and asset depth matter; use Beautiful.ai when slide consistency and viewer insights matter most.
